The New England Forest project will reforest the region by planting native trees such as maple, oak and birch to enhance natural beauty and create habitat for wildlife while mitigating the effects of climate change. The project is led by a coalition of groups and aims to involve the local community.

The Northern Forest project aims to create a contiguous forest area over a vast area in the US that will sequester carbon emissions and provide habitat for wildlife. It is led by a coalition of groups.

The Siberian Forest project will restore Siberian forests destroyed by logging and forest fires and plant native tree species to create jobs and economic opportunities for local people.

The Green Belt project in China aims to combat desertification, reduce air pollution and create habitat for wildlife by planting a huge belt of trees in northern China. It is being implemented by the Chinese government with the support of local and international organisations.

The Scandinavian Forest project focuses on reforesting the boreal forests in Scandinavia, planting native tree species and introducing sustainable forestry practises for the benefit of local communities and the environment.

The Himalayan Forest project aims to reforest the Himalayan region by planting indigenous tree species and introducing sustainable land management practises that promote biodiversity and benefit local communities.
